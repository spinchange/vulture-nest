# The Zettelkasten Method

The Zettelkasten (German for "slip box") is a decentralized knowledge management system designed to turn reading and research into a "conversation" with your notes.

## Core Principles
*   **Atomicity:** Each note (Zettel) should contain exactly one idea or concept. This makes notes modular and easier to link.
*   **Autonomy:** Each note must be self-contained and understandable without its original context.
*   **Connectivity:** The value of a note is determined by its links. A note is only useful if it is connected to other notes in the system.
*   **No Hierarchy:** Avoid rigid folders. Use tags and direct links to allow a "bottom-up" structure to emerge naturally.

## The Note Types
1.  **Fleeting Notes:** Quick reminders or captures. These are temporary and should be processed or deleted within a day or two.
2.  **Literature Notes:** Summaries of things you read/watch, written in your own words. They include bibliographic data.
3.  **Permanent Notes:** The "gold" of the system. These are refined, atomic ideas derived from your literature notes, fully integrated into the Zettelkasten with links to existing notes.

## The Workflow
1.  **Capture:** Write fleeting notes as you encounter ideas.
2.  **Process:** Convert fleeting/literature notes into permanent notes.
3.  **Link:** Find existing notes that relate to the new one. Ask: "How does this idea support, contradict, or expand on what I already know?"
4.  **Retrieve:** When writing or researching, follow the "trails" of links to discover unexpected connections and insights.
