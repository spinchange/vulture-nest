* Photuris* is a

[genus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genus)of

[fireflies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Firefly)(

[beetles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beetle)of the

[family](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Family_(biology))Lampyridae). The adult females of this genus are notable for preying on other fireflies. They engage in

[aggressive mimicry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aggressive_mimicry), imitating the light signals that other firefly

[species](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Species)' females use to attract mates – but

*Photuris*use it to attract, kill and eat the unsuspecting males of those other species. Their flashing

[bioluminescent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bioluminescent)signals seem to have

[evolved](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evolved)independently and eventually

[adapted](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adaptation)to those of their prey, mainly unrelated

[Lampyrinae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lampyrinae), such as

*(rover fireflies) or*

[Photinus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photinus_(beetle))*.*

[Pyractomena](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyractomena)

[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org#cite_note-1)At least 64 species are currently recognized, [2] all restricted to temperate North America.

They mainly occur from the East Coast to Texas.
