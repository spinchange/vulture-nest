# Community Report: PKM & Personal Knowledge Management

**Context:** This report synthesizes the historical and methodological foundations of **Personal Knowledge Management (PKM)** within the vault. It connects 15 notes spanning from 20th-century visionary concepts to modern digital implementation frameworks.

## 1. Visionary Foundations

The vault traces the lineage of PKM back to the concept of **Human Intellect Augmentation**.

*   **The Memex:** [[memex]] serves as the theoretical precursor to modern hyperlinked systems, envisioning a device that supplements human memory.
*   **Augmentation:** [[augmenting-human-intellect]] and [[collective-iq]] explore Doug Engelbart's vision of using technology to solve increasingly complex problems.

## 2. Core Methodologies

The collection highlights several distinct approaches to organizing thought and action.

*   **GTD:** [[gtd]] (Getting Things Done) provides the framework for task management and "actionable" capture.
*   **Zettelkasten:** This is a major pillar, with notes on [[zettelkasten]], [[zettelkasten-workflow]], and specific [[zettelkasten-note-types]] (Fleeting, Literature, Permanent).
*   **Gardening:** [[knowledge-gardening-principles]] emphasizes the iterative process of tending to notes over time rather than simple storage.

## 3. Structural Paradigms

Notes distinguish between different ways of structuring information.

*   **Actionable vs. Reference:** [[actionable-vs-reference]] defines the split between project-based tasks and static knowledge.
*   **Hierarchical vs. Networked:** The use of [[outliners]] and [[org-mode]] represents structured hierarchy, while [[roam-research]] and its descendants focus on networked thought.

## 4. Software Landscape

The [[pkm-software-landscape]] maps the evolution of tools that facilitate these methods, ranging from text-based systems to modern graph-based databases.
